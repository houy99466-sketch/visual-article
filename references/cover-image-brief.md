# Cover Image Brief

Generate a separate homepage or push-card cover image after drafting the article. The image should be a hook, not a title slide.

## Purpose

The cover image should make someone want to open the article by showing:

- A pain point being resolved
- A before/after transformation
- A complete framework or workflow
- A vivid user scene
- The result of the system in action

## Avoid

- Do not simply repeat the article title.
- Do not make a generic abstract technology image.
- Do not rely on tiny unreadable text.
- Do not include unsupported logos, brand marks, or copyrighted characters.

## Good Concepts

For a conversation-to-article automation, a strong cover might show:

- A messy stack of chat bubbles flowing into a clean visual article and a separate cover card.
- A creator facing scattered notes on one side and a polished publishing package on the other.
- A pipeline where raw context becomes market framing, solution, diagrams, case story, future outlook, and cover image.

## Prompt Shape

When using image generation, write a prompt that includes:

- The concrete transformation or scene
- The emotional hook
- The visual metaphor
- The desired aspect ratio or composition
- A note to avoid duplicating the article title

Example:

```text
Create a polished editorial cover image showing a messy software/product conversation transforming into a clean publish-ready article package: HTML page, diagrams, and a separate homepage image card. Emphasize the relief of turning scattered context into a clear story. No large headline text, no duplicated article title, modern product editorial style, high contrast, clear focal point.
```
